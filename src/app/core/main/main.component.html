In order to increase the customer experience, our team has decided on several key technologies that will help achieve the goals of our User Interface. The customer will have a single-entry point to our platform, one that is housed in an angular bootstrap designed website. Bootstrap will create a seamless user-experience, which will automatically generate a GUI specific to the device and browser type. With the increased technology available on mobile devices, high-quality images can be taken from any location, loaded to our service, and the user will receive an immediate response.

Once the customer obtains access to our service through Google and Google Firebase Authentication, they will have the ability to select an image from their device to upload for decryption. The purpose of our service is to read handwritten text, though typed text can still be leveraged. Our first challenge is to obtain the text from the image into a format that can be manipulated for further decryption. After plenty of research and concept creation, our team has settled on leveraging the Google Vision API. The services it provides, while maintaining the stakeholder requirements is unmatched for the low maintenance cost.

Once we can detect the text from an image using OCR, we'll need to take the text and run it through our decryption methods with brute-forced patterns. Our team has decided to tackle the three most common encryption techniques: Caesar Cipher, AES and Triple DES. Caesar Cipher leverages a standard shift key of three, while AES and Triple DES do need a passphrase to decrypt. Our service does provide a convenient Encryption form for testing these services.

As our service begins to decrypt the message, each potential outcome is stored in a database for the analysis phase, sorted by the user id. At this point, we are unaware of which outcome is the correct one, nor do we know the language it originated from. Each possible outcome will be sent to a multi-language dictionary service for verification. The results from the service should be able to validate if the specific combination of letters and words corresponds with a word in its designated language. Once we receive a positive response for every word in the original message, we will be able to tag it as the answer. Supported languages are the following: English, Spanish, and French.

Who are the primary stakeholders for this application?

Potential users of the application are international security teams who use the application to open and decode messages from foreign accounts/malicious users.

What is the scope of the project?

The nature of this project is to create a working prototype of an entire system that links 'off the shelf' (OTS) technologies together in order to digitize and decrypt handwritten encoded messages. The system involves a set of linked procedures that takes a handwritten encrypted message and digitizes it. Then, it gets decrypted and solved.

What is encryption?

Encryption is a way of scrambling data so that only authorized parties can understand the information. In technical terms, it is the process of converting human-readable plaintext to incomprehensible text, also known as ciphertext. In simpler terms, encryption takes readable data and alters it so that it appears random.

What is decryption?

The conversion of encrypted data into its original form is called Decryption. It is generally a reverse process of encryption. It decodes the encrypted information so that an authorized user can only decrypt the data because decryption requires a secret key or password.

What is an OCR?

Optical character recognition, the conversion of images of typed, handwritten, or printed text into machine-encoded text.

What is a COT?

Commercial off-the-shelf technologies available for integration efforts.
